# Style Guide

## Core Direction

The story should feel like a prestige corporate thriller and family dynasty drama.

The business leak is the plot engine, but the real story is Ethan realizing Marcus did not just frame him. Marcus has been waiting years to replace him.

Use a cold, expensive, corporate-noir look: controlled wealth, quiet cruelty, and family betrayal staged like a boardroom execution.

## Tone References

- `Succession`: family power, emotional cruelty, wealthy restraint
- `Michael Clayton`: grounded legal-thriller realism
- `Billions`: glossy finance and legal-world polish
- `The Social Network`: ambition, betrayal, sharp interiors
- `Severance`: clean corporate unease, but not science fiction

These are style anchors only. Do not copy specific characters, scenes, logos, or dialogue.

## Visual Language

- Glass law offices
- Dark suits
- Muted navy, charcoal, black, steel gray
- Cold daylight through office windows
- Warm practical lamps in private offices
- Shallow depth of field
- Tense closeups
- Phones, printed emails, folders, doorways, conference tables
- Restrained performances instead of melodrama
- Family hierarchy shown through blocking: who stands beside Richard, who sits in the inherited chair, who is left outside the room

## AI Generation Strengths

Current image models should handle this style well because it relies on clear, recognizable elements:

- Distinct archetypes: powerful father, younger son, older brother
- Polished corporate interiors
- Simple blocking: one person seated, one standing, one watching
- Closeups with strong lighting
- Recognizable props: phone, printed email, cardboard box, glass office, parking garage
- Emotional reactions instead of complex legal or business action

Keep the business crisis implied through phone screens, headlines, printed emails, voicemails, and facial reactions.

## Prompt Anchors

Use this base style language for office shots:

```text
cinematic corporate thriller, prestige television drama, cold glass law office, tense family confrontation, shallow depth of field, muted navy and charcoal color palette, realistic lighting, vertical 9:16, dramatic closeup
```

Use this base style language for parking garage shots:

```text
cinematic corporate thriller, underground parking garage, cold concrete, harsh fluorescent lighting, tense brother confrontation, vertical 9:16, realistic drama, shallow depth of field
```

## Character Visual Direction

### Ethan Vale

- Younger and polished, but slightly vulnerable
- Good suit, but less severe than Marcus
- Expressive face
- Should look like he expected approval and got punished instead

### Marcus Vale

- Older, colder, sharper
- More severe suit
- Controlled posture
- Should look like he has already won before Ethan understands the game

### Richard Vale

- Powerful founder energy
- Conservative expensive suit
- Emotionally contained
- Should look like a man who treats family pain as a business decision

## Shot Preferences

Prioritize:

- Closeups
- Over-the-shoulder shots
- Phone insert shots
- Doorway framing
- Conference table power blocking
- Parking garage confrontation
- Low-angle final shot of Ethan looking back at the firm

Avoid:

- Crowded scenes
- Courtrooms
- Big action
- Too many background characters
- Complex screens with lots of text
- Wide shots with too much visual detail
- Bright sitcom lighting
- Fantasy, sci-fi, or comic-book styling

## Practical Rule

Make it feel like a family betrayal thriller disguised as a corporate crisis.
