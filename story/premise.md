# Premise

## Title

The Leak

## Core Idea

Ethan Vale, the younger son of a powerful law firm founder, believes his father is finally calling him in to discuss a promotion. Instead, Richard Vale fires him in front of the family firm after a confidential Northstar Petroleum offshore liability memo leaks from Ethan's account.

Ethan thinks he is losing his future over a mistake he did not make. His older brother, Marcus, knows the truth: he leaked the memo to destroy Ethan's favored-son status and force their father to see who really deserves the Vale legacy.

The business crisis creates the pressure, but the story is about two brothers fighting over inheritance, approval, and the right to carry the family name.

## Short Logline

When a confidential memo leaks from his account, Ethan Vale is fired by his own father and stripped of his future at the family law firm, only to discover his older brother Marcus framed him to steal their father's trust.

## Format

- Target duration: 30-60 seconds
- Aspect ratio: 9:16
- Target platform: vertical short-form video
- Genre: family corporate thriller
- Tone: tense, polished, personal

## Core Conflict

- Ethan thinks his father is finally choosing him, then realizes he has been sacrificed.
- Marcus thinks he is correcting years of favoritism by proving Ethan cannot survive pressure.
- Richard thinks protecting the Vale name requires choosing one son over the other.

## Hook

Ethan walks into his father's office expecting a promotion. Richard closes the door and fires him for a leak Ethan did not know happened.

## Ending

Ethan discovers proof that Marcus accessed the document, but exposing him means turning a private brotherly betrayal into a public war for the Vale family legacy.

## Next Episode Hook

Richard asks Marcus to step into Ethan's place at the firm, giving Marcus exactly what he wanted. Ethan now has evidence his older brother framed him, but using it will force him to decide whether he wants his father's approval back or wants to burn down the lie that held the family together.

## Settings

Use only two settings:

1. Law firm office / conference room
2. Parking garage / elevator lobby

Northstar Petroleum, reporters, board, HR, and media are implied through phone alerts, headlines, voicemails, and offscreen pressure. The emotional focus stays on Ethan, Marcus, and Richard.
