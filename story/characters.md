# Characters

## Ethan Vale

- Role in story: younger son, junior employee at his father's law firm, and presumed heir
- Personality: smart, underestimated, eager to prove he deserves the family name
- Visual description: polished but young, still trying to look like he belongs in rooms built by older men
- Wardrobe: tailored office clothes, slightly less sharp than the senior partners
- Key props: phone, sealed Northstar Petroleum file, cardboard box after being fired
- Voice or speech style: quick, defensive, emotional when cornered; he speaks like someone still trying to earn approval
- Consistency notes: he is not fully innocent of privilege, but he is innocent of the leak

## Marcus Vale

- Role in story: older brother who frames Ethan to take his place
- Personality: ambitious, resentful, strategic, more qualified on paper
- Visual description: composed, precise, always watching from the edge of the room
- Wardrobe: sharper and more severe than Ethan's, signaling seniority and control
- Key props: Ethan's desk, Ethan's account, email to a financial reporter
- Voice or speech style: slower, colder, and more formal than Ethan; he speaks like an older brother used to correcting him
- Consistency notes: he believes Ethan is being handed a future he had to fight for

## Richard Vale

- Role in story: father of Ethan and Marcus, founder and managing partner of Vale & Rowe
- Personality: powerful, image-conscious, terrified of losing the firm and the family legacy
- Visual description: authoritative, expensive, visibly shaken when the crisis hits
- Wardrobe: senior partner suit, conservative and immaculate
- Key props: phone, printed email, conference room table
- Voice or speech style: clipped, commanding, emotionally restrained until forced to choose
- Consistency notes: he may love Ethan, but he chooses the Vale name and the firm first
